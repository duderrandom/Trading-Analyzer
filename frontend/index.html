How it works

Moving Average Crossover

Go long when the short SMA crosses above the long SMA; flat otherwise. Trend-following: great in sustained trends, gets whipsawed in chop.

RSI Mean Reversion

Enter long when RSI drops below the oversold threshold; exit when RSI rises above overbought. Opposite philosophy from the MA strategy.

Buy & Hold

Baseline. Any active strategy that can't beat buy-and-hold on this window isn't adding value here.

Understanding the metrics

Total return

Cumulative gain: final / initial − 1. Easy to compare, ignores duration and volatility. +50% over 1 year ≠ +50% over 10 years.

Annualized (CAGR)

Constant yearly growth rate producing the same final equity: (final/initial)^(1/years) − 1. The right number for comparing across different window lengths. Past CAGR is a description, not a forecast.

Max drawdown

Largest peak-to-trough equity decline on the window. This is a risk metric — it tells you the worst it felt. A −50% drawdown needs +100% to recover. If you couldn't live through it, the return doesn't matter.

Sharpe ratio

Return per unit of volatility, annualized: mean(excess) / stdev(excess) × √252. Rule of thumb: <0.5 weak, 0.5–1 decent, 1–2 good, >2 suspicious (probably overfit). Treats upside as risk; assumes normal returns.

Win rate

Fraction of completed BUY→SELL round-trips that made money. Does not imply profitability. A 30%-win trend strategy with big winners beats a 90%-win one with huge losses. Descriptive, not evaluative. Shows "—" when no trades have closed.

Number of trades

Proxy for turnover. Under ~20 round-trips, results are mostly noise — any outcome is luck. High turnover magnifies the costs we don't charge by default.

Reading them together
  1. Drawdown first — if unlivable, return is irrelevant.
  2. Then CAGR — normalizes for window length.
  3. Then Sharpe — did the return justify the ride?
  4. Check trade count — too few = premature conclusion.
  5. Win rate is for curiosity, not decisions.

Limitations

What this tool ignores
  • No taxes. Default no commissions/slippage (opt in above).
  • All-in / all-out sizing only.
  • Single ticker — survivorship bias on names that still trade today.
  • In-sample results on a fixed window; not predictive of future returns.
